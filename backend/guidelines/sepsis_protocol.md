# Sepsis and Septic Shock Management Protocol

## 1. Early Recognition
Sepsis should be suspected in patients presenting with a confirmed or suspected infection and a sudden deterioration in vital signs.
Key indicators (qSOFA criteria):
- Respiratory rate >= 22/min
- Altered mentation
- Systolic blood pressure <= 100 mmHg

Other vital sign anomalies:
- Heart rate > 90 bpm
- Temperature > 38.3°C or < 36°C

## 2. Immediate Interventions (The 1-Hour Bundle)
If sepsis is suspected and the patient is exhibiting severe vital instability (e.g. HR > 110, BP < 90/60):
1. **Measure Lactate Level**: Remeasure if initial lactate is > 2 mmol/L.
2. **Obtain Blood Cultures**: Must be done prior to administration of antibiotics.
3. **Administer Broad-Spectrum Antibiotics**: Recommended: Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV.
4. **Administer IV Fluids**: Begin rapid administration of 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate >= 4 mmol/L.
5. **Apply Vasopressors**: If hypotensive during or after fluid resuscitation to maintain MAP >= 65 mmHg. Recommended: Norepinephrine starting at 0.02 - 0.05 mcg/kg/min.
