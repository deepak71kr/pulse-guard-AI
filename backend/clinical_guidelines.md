# Sepsis and Septic Shock Management
## Suspected Sepsis
When a patient exhibits signs of suspected sepsis (e.g., meeting qSOFA criteria with tachycardia and hypotension), immediately initiate the sepsis bundle.
**Recommended Protocol:** Administer Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV & 30mL/kg fluid bolus.
**Dosage:** 4.5g IV over 30 mins.

# Respiratory Distress
## Hypoxia
When a patient exhibits severe hypoxia (SpO2 < 92%), immediate oxygenation is required to prevent tissue damage.
**Recommended Protocol:** Initiate supplemental oxygen via nasal cannula or non-rebreather mask.
**Dosage:** 2-6 L/min titration to maintain SpO2 > 94%.

# Cardiac Arrhythmias
## Bradycardia
When a patient exhibits symptomatic bradycardia (Heart Rate < 60 bpm with hypotension or altered mental status), intervention is necessary to increase cardiac output.
**Recommended Protocol:** Administer Atropine.
**Dosage:** 1mg IV every 3-5 mins up to 3mg max.
